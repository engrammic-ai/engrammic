Beachhead, not the market. The market is agent memory infrastructure. These startups are the leading indicator for a larger wave (every software company adds agents in 2-3 years).

## Differentiation (hold for when asked)

**Short version:** "Others store memories. We adjudicate claims."

**On Mem0:** "Mem0 makes one agent feel personalized. The moment you have two agents sharing context, or a team around them, their architecture breaks. They started single-user; we started org-first."

**On Zep:** "Temporal storage is table stakes now. The question is what you do with it. Zep stores facts with timestamps. We form conclusions from facts and track when those conclusions change."

**On "just use RAG":** "RAG retrieves documents. We track what's true. Different category."

## Vision (hold for moat/why-us section)

Manifold: latent-native memory for world model agents (JEPA, VLAs). Same epistemic primitives, positioned for the next wave of agent architectures. Nobody else is building this.

## IC Presentation Script (loose)

**Slide 1 (Title)**
"Engrammic. We're building the knowledge layer that lets agents actually learn over time."

**Slide 2 (What's Wrong)**
"AI agents are everywhere. Every AI-native team building agents or building with them hits the same exact issue over and over again: retrieval just isn't enough. Agents don't need context, they need knowledge. Knowledge about what's true, where it came from, and when and how it changed.

At Engrammic we've built a knowledge layer that not only gives them that but also allows agents to actually learn and grow over time. The more you use your agents, the better they become."

**Slide 3 (The Problem)**
"Let me put some numbers on this.

Lost context: accuracy drops 30% when crucial context gets drowned in noise. LLMs forget. Every conversation starts from zero.

Bad coordination: multi-agent systems fail 41 to 87 percent of the time in production. 79% of those failures are coordination and shared understanding errors.

And costs: token costs dropped 280x, but enterprise AI bills grew 320%. The more agents you run, the more you re-prompt."

**Slide 4 (The Solution)**
"Engrammic is a plug-and-play knowledge layer. Facts cite their sources. Conclusions update when evidence changes. And it all plugs into any agent stack."

**Slide 5 (How we Solve this)**
"Three pieces under the hood.

First, four-layer cognition: raw observations turn into structured findings with provenance. Outdated info gets replaced automatically.

Second, shared context: agents retain what they learn across sessions, tasks, and teams.

Third, scalable memory: background processes synthesize, prune, and validate. High-value data stays. Noise fades. Everything versioned and traceable."

**Slide 6 (Market Opportunity)**
"The AI orchestration and memory market is around $10 billion in 2026, growing 39% annually. Our serviceable market is about 7,500 AI-native companies at $12K average contract value. That's $100 million plus. We're targeting 20% of that initially."

**Slide 7 (Why Now)**

**Slide 8 (What's missing)**
"Here's what competitors offer versus us. Multi-agent sharing, org-level scoping - we're not alone there. But traceability and self-awareness? That's where we're different. Nobody else tracks where knowledge came from and how it changed over time. We do."

**Slide 9 (Go-to-market)**
"We're targeting AI-first organizations, 10 to 100 engineers, running multi-agent in production today. Two motions: vertical clients running 20+ agent flows, and horizontal partners like agent orchestration platforms who can plug us in for their customers."

LLM agnostic - works with any model. Multi-agent ready from day one. Scales without wasting compute. Delivers consistent, reliable output. And plugs into existing stacks with minimal setup."
