# Positioning: Pain Openers

Status: Draft
Date: 2026-05-21

## Context

ICP: "The next 10-100 AI-native startups or teams building with agents"
One-liner: "The knowledge layer that allows agents to learn like humans do"

Feedback: Nordic investors want simple, scenario-driven explanations. Lead with pain, not architecture. Save "epistemic rigor" for moat/differentiation questions.

## Pain Opener Candidates

### A) The repetition angle
"Your AI makes the same mistake twice. It solved this exact problem last week — today it fumbles it again."

### B) The expertise angle
"Six months in, your AI assistant still doesn't know how you work. It hasn't gotten better at its job."

### C) The signal vs noise angle
"Your AI treats a rumor the same as a verified fact. Everything it 'remembers' has equal weight."

### D) The compounding angle
"Human employees get better over time. Your AI agents don't. Every interaction starts from scratch understanding."

## Notes

- C and D closest to actual problem space without jargon
- D sets up "learn like humans" one-liner naturally
- Need to validate: which pain is truest to early user complaints?

## Outcome Framings (for prospects not feeling pain directly)

### A) The expertise outcome
"After 100 customer conversations, your agent knows which objections actually matter and which ones are noise. It develops expertise — not just logs."

### B) The institutional knowledge outcome
"When your best employee leaves, their knowledge walks out the door. With Engrammic, your agents retain what they learn — across sessions, across team members."

### C) The leverage outcome
"One agent learns something, every agent knows it. Your AI workforce compounds knowledge instead of each one starting from scratch."

### D) The trust outcome
"Agents that can tell you WHY they believe something, not just what they retrieved. That's the difference between a search engine and a colleague."

## Demo Ideas

Challenges:
- Graph visualization shows hairballs unless rendered pretty
- Long-term continuity demos need weeks of data we don't have
- "I don't know" demo not impressive enough on its own

Promising direction: **Live CoT visualization**
- Show agent receiving a query
- Expose the agent's reasoning as it calls Engrammic MCP tools
- Visualize: recall -> reasoning -> learn cycle
- Real-time "watch the cognitive process" rather than static graph
